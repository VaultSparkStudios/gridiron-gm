Executive Summary

Strategic Verdict
Gridiron GM is not playing the same game as Football-GM. Football-GM is a spreadsheet sim — deep, free, and stat-obsessed. Gridiron GM is a franchise experience — live gameplay, narrative arcs, emotional moments. The correct strategic posture is not to out-simulate Football-GM. It is to make Football-GM players feel like something is missing when they return to it.

Football-GM (ZenGM) is the dominant free browser football franchise simulation game, with 1.46 million annual sessions, 2.97 million seasons simulated, and 95 years of cumulative play time in 2025. It is technically solid, actively developed by a solo developer, and has a loyal community of simulation purists who have been playing for 7+ years.

Gridiron GM at v30.0 is a newer, smaller, and more ambitious product. It matches Football-GM on franchise simulation depth, exceeds it significantly on narrative and emotional design, and has a genuine structural moat in one area Football-GM cannot enter: real-time interactive gameplay via the Phaser 3 engine.

The risk is not competitive feature parity. The risk is discoverability, mobile experience, and monetization — three areas where Football-GM's 7-year head start is most visible.

Competitive Weaknesses: Football-GM

These are not speculative gaps — they are structural limitations of the product's design philosophy and solo developer model.

1. No Interactive Gameplay — Structural Limitation

Football-GM simulates. You watch numbers change. There is no play-calling, no real-time control, no player agency during the game. The "play-by-play" is a text log. This is a permanent competitive gap — adding a real-time Phaser engine would require a complete platform rebuild.

2. Zero Narrative or Emotional Layer

Football-GM has no story. No beat reporter, no press conference, no locker room speech, no parade, no GM reputation arc, no player personality archetypes driving drama. Players are ratings, not characters. Sessions end when you're done looking at numbers. There is no "moment" that makes you call your friend and say "you won't believe what just happened in my franchise."

3. Visual Presentation is Purely Utilitarian

The UI is React/Bootstrap — functional, readable, and completely emotionally flat. No animations. No celebration sequences. Draft picks are processed with no ceremony. Championships produce no visual moment. The game is a spreadsheet dressed in a web frame.

4. Solo Developer — Single Point of Failure

Jeremy's second son was born January 1, 2026. Update frequency measurably slowed. A newborn, an illness, or burnout creates a hard stop for the entire platform. No team, no roadmap, no sprint velocity. Football-GM cannot commit to features the way a studio can.

5. Football is the #2 Priority

Basketball-GM drives 3.4x the sessions of Football-GM and has 18 years of development depth. Football-GM inherits shared infrastructure improvements but football-specific features consistently lag. OPOY (Offensive Player of the Year) was only added in March 2026 — six years after Football-GM launched. This is the speed of a secondary product.

6. No Mobile Native App

Responsive web, no App Store / Google Play. PWA installation is available but not marketed. No push notifications. No offline-first design. Mobile users get a functional but suboptimal experience with no upgrade path.

7. AI Trade Logic Is Gameable

The developer himself acknowledged that the AI's trade valuation system is predictable. Power users know how to fleece it. The community asks for better AI, not better UI filters — but this requires significant sim engine work that hasn't shipped.

8. No Multiplayer of Any Kind

Single-player only. No online leagues, no commissioner tools, no head-to-head, no async multiplayer draft. This is an explicit non-goal for the product. The community has asked for it repeatedly. It will not come from Football-GM.

9. Monetization Ceiling Is Capped

All games are permanently free. Ad + Patreon covers one developer's salary. This means Football-GM cannot hire a team, cannot build a mobile app, cannot add a multiplayer backend. The revenue model is structurally incompatible with growth beyond a solo product.

Target Audience Mapping

Football-GM's Core Audience

  • Simulation purists: Want deep stat tracking, AV scores, historical data — don't need "fun," need accuracy
  • Modders & power users: God Mode enthusiasts who want total control; import/export league files
  • Stat-obsessed fans: Cross-reference their franchise stats with real NFL numbers; want graphable historical data
  • Casual browsers: Land via Google, play one session, may not return — low acquisition friction drives volume

Gridiron GM's Natural Audience

  • Experience-seekers: Want to feel the franchise, not just manage a spreadsheet — narrative, moments, personality
  • Active game players: Want to actually call plays and control outcomes in live games
  • Story builders: Dynasty builders who want their franchise to have an arc, a voice, a history with texture
  • Mobile-first casual GMs: Want a great GM experience on their phone — currently underserved by both products
  • Football-GM alumni: Players who've mastered Football-GM and want more depth, more story, more game

Conversion Opportunity

The highest-value target for Gridiron GM is the Football-GM power user who is bored. They've built three dynasties, they know the AI trade logic inside out, they want something that makes the franchise feel alive. This player will convert if: (1) they hear about Gridiron GM, (2) the first session has a moment Football-GM can never give them, and (3) their progress is preserved when they come back.

Strategic Recommendations

Priority 1: Neutralize the Friction Gap

Football-GM's biggest weapon is zero friction — play in 10 seconds. Gridiron GM's onboarding is better than most games but the landing page needs an instant-play mode: "Start with a random team, skip setup." The first 60 seconds of Gridiron GM should contain a moment that Football-GM cannot replicate.

Priority 2: Mobile is the Market

Football-GM is responsive. Gridiron GM's tables break on mobile. This is the single highest-leverage technical fix available — not because mobile users spend more, but because mobile is where discovery happens. A YouTube short of a live Phaser play watched on a phone leads to a Google search on a phone leads to a broken table = lost player. Fix the tables, add bottom nav, and the conversion funnel closes.

Priority 3: Enable Analytics Immediately

The VITE_ANALYTICS_URL is a placeholder. Every session played right now is invisible. Deploy Plausible or Umami (both privacy-safe, one-hour deploy) and immediately gain session counts, retention curves, funnel drop-offs, and feature usage heatmaps. Every future decision becomes data-driven.

Priority 4: Build One Community Anchor

Football-GM has years of Reddit community inertia. Gridiron GM needs one anchor: a subreddit or Discord where players share dynasty stories, draft grades, and highlight cards. The Shareable Season Recap Card (already buildable) is the viral hook — players share, community forms around shares, community creates more players.

Priority 5: One Revenue Line

Football-GM is permanently free and will never charge. This is their constraint and Gridiron GM's opportunity. A "Pro GM" tier at $4.99/month — cosmetic team themes, extra save slots, exclusive stat dashboards — signals premium quality and enables studio investment. The product is polished enough to charge for extras today.

Priority 6: Close the Stats Depth Gap

Football-GM's power users want data. OL stats (PBWR/RBWR), graphable historical charts, career leaderboards, EPA-style metrics — these features signal seriousness to simulation purists. A Stats Hub tab using already-tracked data would take one session to build and dramatically improve retention for the audience most likely to pay.

Conclusion

Final Assessment
Gridiron GM is not behind Football-GM. It is ahead in the dimensions that matter most for the next decade of sports gaming: interactivity, narrative, and emotional engagement. Football-GM is the past — a masterclass in the spreadsheet-sim genre. Gridiron GM is the category that comes after it. The work now is not building more features. It is making Gridiron GM findable, playable on any device, and financially self-sustaining so the studio can keep building.

The 30-feature v30.0 ship represents feature completion for the "manage AND play" prototype. The next phase is platform — community, mobile, monetization, and discoverability. Football-GM's 7-year head start in community cannot be closed with features. It can only be bypassed by building a different community around a different kind of game.

Gridiron GM is that different kind of game. The opportunity is to make the world know it.
